Jun Dai
Jun Dai, academic backbone of South Lake Scholar of Hubei University of Technology. He has long been engaged in the research of functional yeast and brewing microorganism. He has presided over one National Natural Science Foundation of China. And published more than 60 papers, including 49 SCI and EI. He is the editor of special issue of Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology; expert member of Food Hygiene and Safety Education Committee of China Medical Education Association; member of Frontiers in Microbiology, Food Bioscience, Current He is a reviewer for Frontiers in Microbiology, Food Bioscience, Current Microbiology, Food Biotechnology, IJSEM, etc.
Lan Yao
Lan Yao has long been engaged in research on functional yeast and biomass energy, etc. She received her PhD degree in fermentation engineering from Shandong University in June 2011, and joined Hubei University of Technology in the same year. In 2016, she went to the University of Tennessee, USA to conduct visiting scholar research. She has presided over one National Natural Science Foundation of China project, participated in two projects, presided over one National Study Abroad Foundation project, and six provincial and ministerial projects, with a total research funding of more than 1 million RMB. She has published 25 SCI and EI indexed scientific papers. She is a reviewer for Frontiers in Energy Research, International Journal of Molecular Sciences, Bioresources, and other journals.
Kevin Schlei
Kevin Schlei is the founder and developer of Bit Shape Software, LLC, where he creates innovative synthesizers for mobile devices. His music app TC-11 pioneered new methods of multi-touch musical performance, and has won accolades by iOS musicians for over a decade. As a musician, Schlei is a founding member of the Milwaukee Laptop Orchestra (MiLO), the Tablet Orchestra (TORCH), and has performed extensively in Milwaukee, Chicago, and New York. His multimedia works have been exhibited in Athens, Brisbane, Utrecht, Shanghai, and Wuhan, and his research has been published in the NIME, ICMC, and SMC journals. Schlei graduated with his Master's Degree in Music Composition & Theory from the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee in 2006. He then joined the staff at UWM where he taught music composition and technology for 13 years, becoming a Senior Lecturer. In 2019, he left to join the staff of the Hubei University of Technology in Wuhan, China, where he teaches in the Visual Communications & Design and Industrial Design schools.
